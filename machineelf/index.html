The Machine Elf

⚫⚫⚫

Play In 3D Space

The Machine Elf is an analog performance instrument with digital control. It senses tilt and angle in 3D space to control the pitch and tone of its sound. It is also able to connect to other musical devices and software over Bluetooth for limitless sonic expression.

"Machine Elf is made out of two laser cut orange acrylic panels for the top and bottom and is held together by two metal plates on the side. The hexagonal shape with rounded front and back edges was decided upon by us to give a sturdy feel, something which can fit in two and can be easily maneuvered in space to control different aspects of the sound."

How It Works

The Machine Elf has a anolog synth what usuing and Arudino 101 to control its pitch and tone base on the movment of the user. Bluetooth also allows these same reading make the Machine Elf a midi controller as well.

"We used Intel's Curie-based Arduino 101 microcontroller which comes with onboard accelerometer, gyroscope and Bluetooth Low Energy connectivity, all the things we required for the Machine Elf. The incoming gyroscope and accelerometer readings go through simple scaling manipulations in real time and these values are output as PWMs (Pulse Width Modulations), which is just a way of getting analog outputs using digital signals. The same values are then converted to MIDI Note On and Note Off messages and these message bytes are sent over to the Bluetooth paired device on a set channel. In our setup, the Machine Elf transmitted these MIDI messages over to a laptop running Ableton Live to produce some extra synth sounds, functioning as any other Class compliant MIDI over Bluetooth device."
